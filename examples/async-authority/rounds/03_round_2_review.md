# Round 2 Investor Review: The Async Authority

## Overview
The shift to a tiered funnel and email capture significantly improves the structural value of the business. However, it introduces a "Complexity Trap" and a "Trust Deficit" that the plan currently ignores.

---

## Objections

### 1. The Faceless Trust Gap (High-Ticket)
- **Probability of Rejection: 80%**
- **Logic**: You are attempting to sell a $497 Mastermind and a $97/mo Community using stock footage. In the "Authority" niche, buyers pay for the track record and presence of the mentor. A "Faceless" brand works for $27 ebooks, but it creates a massive credibility barrier for high-ticket coaching. 
- **Data**: Analysis of top coaching brands (e.g., Hormozi, Godard) shows that personal brand equity is the primary driver of high-ticket conversion.

### 2. Operational Overload (The "Founder Burnout" Risk)
- **Probability of Rejection: 85%**
- **Logic**: You have moved from a "set and forget" ManyChat link to a complex business requiring: content creation, email copywriting, mastermind delivery, and community moderation. This is no longer a "Faceless Growth Engine"; it is a full-time operations-heavy business. A solo-founder will fail at this scale without a documented "Productized Service" model.

### 3. Funnel Drop-off (The Email Wall)
- **Probability of Rejection: 65%**
- **Logic**: Adding an email capture step introduces "Lead Friction." While it builds an asset, your $9,700/mo front-end revenue estimate assumes 100% conversion parity with your old model. Statistical reality: Email walls reduce immediate link clicks by 50%+. Your revenue math may be over-optimistic by 2x.
